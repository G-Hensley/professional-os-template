# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Launch Timeline

### Phase 1: Pre-Launch (January 1-15, 2026)

**Goals:**
- Build email list of 200+ interested developers
- Create anticipation and social proof
- Validate messaging

**Actions:**
1. **Lead Magnet:** Free "AI Context Guide" - How to make AI tools understand your full context
   - PDF or Notion doc
   - Captures email for launch notification
   - Demonstrates expertise

2. **Content Blitz:**
   - 3 LinkedIn posts per week about the problem/solution
   - 1 longer-form piece (blog or LinkedIn article)
   - Daily engagement in dev communities

3. **Landing Page:**
   - Simple page with value prop, email capture
   - "Be first to know when Professional OS launches"
   - Show what's coming (screenshots, feature list)

4. **Community Seeding:**
   - Share insights in relevant Discord/Slack communities
   - Don't sellâ€”educate and help
   - Build reputation as someone who solved this problem

### Phase 2: Launch Week (January 16-22, 2026)

**Goals:**
- 25+ sales in first week
- Generate social proof (testimonials, tweets)
- Create urgency

**Actions:**
1. **Email Sequence:**
   - Day 1: "It's live + 40% off"
   - Day 3: "Here's what early buyers are saying"
   - Day 5: "Launch pricing ends in 48 hours"
   - Day 7: "Last chance for launch pricing"

2. **Social Campaign:**
   - Launch announcement thread (Twitter/LinkedIn)
   - Daily updates on sales/feedback
   - Repost customer reactions

3. **Direct Outreach:**
   - Personal message to 20 people who might benefit
   - Ask for feedback, not just sales
   - Offer to help with setup

4. **Community Announcements:**
   - Share in relevant communities (with permission)
   - Offer launch discount to community members

---

### Phase 3: Post-Launch (February 2026+)

**Goals:**
- Steady sales of 10-20/month
- Build case studies
- Iterate on product based on feedback

**Actions:**
1. **Content Marketing:**
   - Weekly LinkedIn posts showing real usage
   - Monthly blog post on professional management topics
   - Customer success stories

2. **SEO Play:**
   - Target "developer resume generator" keywords
   - "AI context for developers"
   - "professional knowledge management"

3. **Product Iteration:**
   - Collect feedback from buyers
   - Add features based on common requests
   - Document in public (builds in public content)

## Messaging Framework

### Primary Message
"Stop re-explaining yourself to AI. Your professional identity, version-controlled."

### Supporting Messages
1. **For Job Seekers:** "Generate tailored resumes in 5 minutes, not 5 hours"
2. **For Builders:** "Your professional brand on autopilot"
3. **For AI Users:** "Make Claude/Copilot 10x more useful with full context"

### Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I can build this myself" | "You could. But will you? And will you maintain it? This is ready now." |
| "It's just files and folders" | "Structure is the value. Plus automations, commands, and documentation." |
| "Too expensive" | "How many hours do you spend on resumes and context? $97 pays for itself in one job application." |
| "I don't use AI tools that much" | "You will. And when you do, your context will be ready." |
